About Bangla WikiConnect

Bangla WikiConnect is a collaborative initiative focused on enhancing and expanding Wikipedia’s content in Bengali. Through a variety of engaging contests, edit-a-thons, and training programs, we aim to develop high-quality, inclusive content on Wikipedia and its sister projects such as Wikiquote, Wikivoyage, Wikibooks, and Wiktionary.

Our mission is to foster a more informed, educated, and active community that contributes to the digital knowledge repository. By providing a platform for new contributors, training aspiring leaders, and promoting content diversity, we ensure the continued growth of the Bangla language on Wikimedia projects.

Our Projects

Each year, we organize multiple events, including writing contests, photo walks, and collaborative training sessions. These projects focus on key areas such as:

    Wiki Loves Children

    Wiki Loves Children (WLC) is an online initiative aimed at improving Wikipedia’s content on children’s issues, rights, and well-being. We strive to make vital information on these topics accessible to a global audience.

    WLC was inspired by alarming statistics from UNICEF, which reports that over half of the world’s children experience severe violence, with South Asia accounting for 64% of these cases. Despite the urgency, Wikipedia lacks sufficient coverage of issues like child abuse and neglect.

    Founded in 2021, WLC began with a successful event on Bengali Wikipedia. Now, we’re back with a renewed mission to raise awareness and empower communities to address these critical issues. Our goal is to highlight the importance of education, prevention, and support in combating violence against children.

    Wikiquote Writing Contest

    The Bangla Wikiquote Writing Contest is an annual event dedicated to enriching Bangla Wikiquote with high-quality quotes from Bengali and international figures. Launched in April 2024, the contest aims to promote collaboration and engage the community in expanding this important knowledge platform.

    The main objectives of the contest are to create verified and meaningful content by adding quotes from renowned personalities, expand the platform’s reach by encouraging diverse participation, and build a strong community of contributors.

    In 2024, the contest saw participation from 80 contributors, who collectively created 823 quote pages. After review, 673 pages were approved, ensuring they met quality standards. These pages contained 474,170 words, showcasing the commitment of contributors. As a result, Bangla Wikiquote surpassed the 1,000-quote milestone, marking a significant achievement for the platform.

    Wikibooks Writing Contest

    The Wikibooks Writing Contest is an annual book-writing competition hosted on Bangla Wikibooks, designed to promote the creation of high-quality, open educational content. The contest encourages diverse contributions and fosters collaborative development of educational materials across various themes.

    The primary objectives of the contest include creating child-friendly educational content under the Wikijunior namespace, documenting Bengali cuisine in the Cookbook namespace, and expanding the platform’s reach by raising awareness and encouraging broader participation.

    In the previous year, the contest resulted in the creation of 717 new pages, greatly enriching the Bangla Wikibooks collection. These pages collectively amounted to 1,148,079 words, highlighting the dedication and contributions of participants.

    Wiktionary Entry Contest

    Wiktionary Entry Contest is an annual initiative on Bangla Wiktionary, aimed at enriching the project with Bengali language entries. Last year, the contest saw remarkable engagement, with 420 users registering to participate. Of these, 43 contributors actively submitted entries, resulting in an impressive 10,302 pages created. After rigorous review, 9,249 pages were accepted, significantly exceeding our initial expectations—almost 10 times the proposed target.

    Wikivoyage Article Writing Contest

    The Wikivoyage Article Writing Contest is an annual event aimed at improving content, building, and retaining an independent and self-governing community that adheres to Wikivoyage's policies. It is hosted on Bangla Wikivoyage to coincide with World Tourism Day. The 2025 edition will mark its third year.

    In last year's competition, participants created 1,000 articles, achieving twenty times the initial target. A total of 355 users registered, but 76 submitted articles using the Fountain tool, 71 of whom were newcomers. The word count on Bangla Wikivoyage increased from 0.47 million to 41 million as a result of the competition. Bangla Wikivoyage’s ranking based on article count rose from 19th to 13th.

    Train the newcomers: a video tutorial project

    A long-standing complaint from newcomers is the lack of video resources to support their training. During every edit-a-thon and outreach project, we repeatedly hear this concern. Although we have made individual attempts in the past, these efforts have failed due to limited resources. Therefore, as part of this project, we plan to create a series of video tutorials organized into a playlist. These videos will be used to train newcomers and help retain them in the community.

    Wiki Loves Women

    We, at Bangla Wikipedia, organize a month-long edit-a-thon every year on Women’s Day to address gender gaps on Bangla Wikipedia. Previous organizers of these edit-a-thons have agreed to incorporate this event under this initiative starting in March 2026. Our goal is to reduce content-related gender gaps and encourage more women to contribute. We plan to collaborate with WikiNondini, a local women’s Wikimedia group, to achieve these objectives.
